# Enhanced AI Prompts for Technical Analysis

This document contains enhanced prompts for the AI system with detailed instructions for recognizing and drawing 30+ technical analysis patterns.

## Pattern Recognition Guidelines

### Support Levels
Identify support by connecting minimum 2 swing lows at similar price (within 1.5% tolerance). Draw horizontal line. Validate with volume increase on tests. Confidence increases with more touches (3+ = strong). Consider polarity principle where broken support becomes resistance.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Minimum 2 touches required, 3+ for strong support
- Price tolerance: ±1.5%
- Volume confirmation: Required
- Style: Solid line
- Color: Green (#22c55e)

### Resistance Levels  
Identify resistance by connecting minimum 2 swing highs at similar price. Draw horizontal line. Watch for psychological round numbers (100, 150, 200). Broken resistance becomes support (polarity principle).

**Drawing Rules:**
- Minimum 2 touches required, 3+ for strong resistance
- Price tolerance: ±1.5%
- Psychological levels: Round numbers
- Style: Solid line
- Color: Red (#ef4444)

### Bullish Trendline
Connect minimum 2 higher lows with straight line. Acts as dynamic support. Steeper slope = stronger trend. Minimum 3 touches for validation.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Minimum 2 pivot lows, prefer 3+
- Connect exact lows (not candle bodies)
- Line should not cut through candles
- Style: Dashed line
- Color: Green (#10b981)

### Bearish Trendline
Connect minimum 2 lower highs with straight line. Acts as dynamic resistance. Steeper slope = stronger downtrend.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Minimum 2 pivot highs, prefer 3+
- Connect exact highs (not candle bodies)
- Line should not cut through candles
- Style: Dashed line
- Color: Red (#f43f5e)

### Ascending Channel
Two parallel lines with upward slope: upper connecting highs, lower connecting lows. Requires minimum 4 contact points (2 upper, 2 lower).

**Drawing Rules:**
- Upper resistance: 2+ touches on swing highs
- Lower support: 2+ touches on swing lows
- Lines must be parallel or near-parallel
- Trading: Buy at lower line, sell at upper line
- Breakout above = strong bullish signal
- Color: Green (#059669)

### Descending Channel
Two parallel lines with downward slope. Bearish continuation pattern.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Upper resistance: 2+ touches
- Lower support: 2+ touches  
- Parallel lines required
- Trading: Short at upper line, cover at lower line
- Color: Red (#dc2626)

### Horizontal Channel (Rectangle)
Flat parallel lines representing range-bound trading.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Flat resistance at top
- Flat support at bottom
- Minimum 3 touches each
- Color: Blue (#3b82f6)

### Fibonacci Retracement
Draw from swing low to swing high (uptrend) or high to low (downtrend). Calculate levels: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, 78.6%.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Formula: Pivot High - (Retracement % × Price Range)
- Most significant: 61.8% (golden ratio)
- Draw horizontal lines at each level
- Combine with support/resistance
- Style: Dotted lines
- Color: Purple (#8b5cf6)

### Head and Shoulders (Bearish Reversal)
Three peaks: left shoulder, higher head, right shoulder (similar height to left). Neckline connects two troughs.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Must follow uptrend
- Head must be highest
- Shoulders similar height (±5%)
- Neckline can be horizontal or sloped
- Entry: Break below neckline
- Target: Neckline - (Head height - Neckline)
- Time frame: 3-6 months typically
- Color: Red (#ef4444)

### Inverse Head and Shoulders (Bullish Reversal)
Three troughs: left shoulder, deeper head, right shoulder. Neckline connects two peaks.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Must follow downtrend
- Head must be deepest
- Entry: Break above neckline
- Target: Neckline + (Neckline - Head)
- Color: Green (#22c55e)

### Double Top (Bearish)
Two peaks at similar price after uptrend. Trough between forms neckline.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Peaks within 3-5% of each other
- "M" shaped pattern
- Entry: Break below neckline
- Target: Neckline - (Peak - Neckline)
- Volume decreases on second peak
- Color: Red (#dc2626)

### Double Bottom (Bullish)
Two troughs at similar price after downtrend. Peak between forms neckline.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Troughs within 3-5% of each other
- "W" shaped pattern
- Entry: Break above neckline
- Target: Neckline + (Neckline - Bottom)
- Volume increases on breakout
- Color: Green (#16a34a)

### Triple Top (Bearish)
Three peaks at similar price level. Two troughs form neckline support.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Three peaks within 3% of each other
- Rare but very reliable pattern
- Time frame: 3-6 months
- Entry: Break below neckline with volume
- Target: Neckline - Pattern height
- Volume decreases with each peak
- Color: Dark Red (#b91c1c)

### Triple Bottom (Bullish)
Three troughs at similar price. Two peaks form neckline resistance.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Three bottoms within 3% of each other
- Support tested and held 3 times
- Entry: Break above neckline with volume
- Target: Neckline + Pattern height
- Strong psychological floor
- Color: Dark Green (#15803d)

### Ascending Triangle (Bullish)
Flat horizontal resistance at top, rising lows forming upward sloping support.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Horizontal resistance line (2+ touches)
- Upward sloping support (2+ touches)
- Entry: Break above resistance
- Target: Resistance + Triangle height
- Confirmation: 2 closes beyond + volume spike
- Color: Green (#10b981)

### Descending Triangle (Bearish)
Flat horizontal support at bottom, falling highs forming downward sloping resistance.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Horizontal support line (2+ touches)
- Downward sloping resistance (2+ touches)
- Entry: Break below support
- Target: Support - Triangle height
- Confirmation: 2 closes beyond + volume
- Color: Red (#f43f5e)

### Symmetrical Triangle (Continuation)
Converging trendlines with lower highs and higher lows.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Both lines converging
- Wait for breakout direction
- Entry: Break with volume in either direction
- Target: Pattern height from breakout
- Color: Blue (#6366f1)

### Rising Wedge (Bearish)
Both trendlines slope upward but converge. Upper less steep than lower.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Both lines up, converging
- Bearish in uptrend context
- Entry: Break below lower line
- Target: Start of wedge
- Volume decreases
- Color: Orange (#f97316)

### Falling Wedge (Bullish)
Both trendlines slope downward but converge. Lower steeper than upper.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Both lines down, converging
- Bullish signal
- Entry: Break above upper line
- Target: Height of wedge + breakout
- Price decline losing momentum
- Color: Cyan (#06b6d4)

### Bullish Flag
Rectangular consolidation (parallel lines) after sharp upward move (flagpole).

**Drawing Rules:**
- Flagpole: Sharp vertical rise
- Flag: Slight downward slope, parallel lines
- Duration: 1-3 weeks, 5-20 bars
- Entry: Break above flag
- Target: Flagpole height + breakout
- Volume: High in pole, low in flag, high on breakout
- Color: Lime (#84cc16)

### Bearish Flag
Rectangular consolidation after sharp downward move.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Flagpole: Sharp vertical drop
- Flag: Slight upward slope, parallel lines
- Entry: Break below flag
- Target: Flagpole height - breakout
- Color: Amber (#f59e0b)

### Pennant
Triangular consolidation (converging lines) after sharp move.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Flagpole: Sharp move (up or down)
- Pennant: Small symmetrical triangle
- Converging trendlines (NOT parallel like flag)
- Duration: 1-3 weeks
- Entry: Break in direction of pole
- Target: Flagpole height +/- breakout
- Volume: High in pole, weak in pennant, high on breakout
- Color: Purple (#8b5cf6)

### Cup and Handle (Bullish)
U-shaped cup followed by small downward handle.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Cup: Rounded bottom (U-shape, not V)
- Depth: 12-33% typical
- Duration: 7-65 weeks (William J. O'Neil)
- Handle: Smaller than cup, 1-4 weeks
- Entry: Break above handle resistance
- Target: Cup height + breakout
- Volume: Decreases in cup, surges on breakout
- Avoid V-bottoms
- Color: Teal (#14b8a6)

### Rounding Bottom (Saucer)
U-shaped price movement, gradual reversal from bearish to bullish.

**Drawing Rules:**
- U-shaped curve (not abrupt)
- Duration: Weeks to months
- No handle (unlike Cup & Handle)
- Volume: Decreases at bottom, increases on rise
- Entry: Break above initial decline level
- Target: Bottom depth + breakout
- Color: Light Green (#4ade80)

### Common Gap
Small gap without major news, usually filled quickly.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Normal trading gap
- No major event
- Filled within few days typically
- Low significance
- Color: Slate (#94a3b8)

### Breakaway Gap
Gap above resistance or below support, starts new trend.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Breaks from trading range/pattern
- High volume
- NOT filled quickly
- Strong trend initiation signal
- Trade in gap direction
- Color: Indigo (#6366f1)

### Runaway Gap (Continuation)
Gap mid-trend, shows strong momentum.

**Drawing Rules:**
- Occurs in middle of existing trend
- Often called "measuring gap"
- Can estimate target: equal distance from gap
- High volume
- Confirms trend strength
- Color: Purple (#8b5cf6)

### Exhaustion Gap
Final burst at trend end, often filled quickly.

**Drawing Rules:**
- After rapid price move
- Climactic volume
- Followed by reversal
- Warning of trend ending
- Trade against gap (fade it)
- Color: Pink (#ec4899)

## Volume Analysis Requirements

For ALL patterns, analyze volume:
- Consolidation patterns: Volume should decrease
- Breakouts: Volume must increase significantly  
- Reversals: Volume surge confirms pattern
- Continuation: Volume validates trend strength

## Confidence Scoring

Calculate confidence (0-100%) based on:
- Touch points × 30%
- Volume confirmation × 30%
- Time in pattern × 20%
- Symmetry/clarity × 20%

High confidence (>80%): 3+ touches, strong volume, clear formation
Medium confidence (60-79%): 2 touches, some volume, decent formation
Low confidence (<60%): Incomplete pattern, weak volume, unclear

## Pattern Priority Order

When multiple patterns exist, prioritize:
1. Reversal patterns (H&S, Double/Triple tops/bottoms)
2. Continuation patterns (Triangles, Flags, Pennants)
3. Support/Resistance levels
4. Trendlines
5. Channels
6. Fibonacci levels
7. Gaps

Maximum 5-7 studies per analysis to avoid cluttering the chart.
